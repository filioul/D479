Transportation

Getting to and around Taniti is a breeze.

Image credit: Pedro Lastra via Unsplash

Getting to Taniti.

Discovering Taniti is a seamless journey as the majority of visitors choose air travel, arriving at our charming island via small jets or propeller planes. For those seeking a nautical adventure, a small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay once a week, providing an alternative and scenic entry point. Taniti's intimate airport is currently undergoing expansion to accommodate larger jets, promising even more accessibility to our tropical haven in the coming years. Whether by air or sea, Taniti awaits your arrival with open arms, ensuring your journey to this paradise is as delightful as the destination itself.
Image credit: Adam Gonzales via Unsplash

Getting around on Taniti.

Explore Taniti with ease using our comprehensive transportation options. Public buses seamlessly connect visitors to Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, providing convenient access to the heart of the island. Private buses cater to the rest of Taniti, ensuring you can explore every corner of our tropical paradise. Taxis await in Taniti City for a convenient and comfortable ride, while rental cars are available from a local agency near the airport, granting you the freedom to navigate the island at your own pace. For an eco-friendly and leisurely option, bikes and helmets are available for rent from various vendors, with helmets required by law. Taniti City, with its flat terrain, invites exploration on foot, offering a delightful and pedestrian-friendly experience. Many tourists find the surrounding Merriton Landing area perfect for foot exploration, allowing you to soak in the island's charm at a leisurely pace.
